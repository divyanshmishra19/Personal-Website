A bit about me

Grad Student working in the field of CS Education, Software Engineering, and Software Testing

I am a thesis-track Grad Student at Virginia Tech Department of Computer Science. I graduated with a bachelors in Information Technology from Manipal Institute of Technology with a specialization in Big Data in June, 2021. I started my MS in CS at VT in August, 2021 and have since worked on multiple projects in the domain of software engineering, CS education, computing and social good, and information and data retrieval.

For my theis, I'm working with Dr. Stephen Edwards on PEML, which stands for program exercise markup language, a markup language designed to make writing CS programming assignment descriptions easily. The project is aimed at increasing the shareability and reusability of programming assignments as well as make automated grading simplified. The PEML tool itself will be provided as a Rails application, a Rest API, and a Ruby gem. The gem is designed to parse PEML descriptions into JSON/YAML descriptions, making it grading-tool neutral. This project is part of the larger SPLICE project at VT.

My Published Work

Cogent Engineering

Hybrid CF-SWRL recommendation system for MOOCs that provides course based and module based recommendations to learners at different granularities based on detected learning style following the FSLSM learning style model. Recommendations validated over the course of one semester with 45 participants to high accuracy.

Progress in Artificial Intelligence

A Rasa-powered chat-bot that can convert natural language in english to SPARQL to query large-scale data-rich knowledge-bases. Utilizes dual intent detection and entity picking to classify intent based queries which are then converted into valid SPARQL queries. Validated with over 2000 randomly generated queries against MIT Hostels konwledge-base.

iJET

Chrome extension that uses learner ontology together with MOOC ontology to automatically and dynamically detect a learner's learning style based on the Felder-Silverman Learning Style Model. Tested for two semesters with 40 participants using Coursera and validated against paper based FSLSM quiz answers.
